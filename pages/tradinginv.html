The competition was structured to simulate real-world trading scenarios, allowing us to act both as hedge funds and market makers. For those unfamiliar, hedge funds and market makers play crucial roles in the financial markets. Hedge funds are investment funds that employ various strategies to maximize returns for their investors. They can invest in a wide range of assets, including equities, bonds, derivatives, and more. The primary goal of a hedge fund is to generate high returns while managing risk, often through sophisticated techniques such as leverage and short selling.

Market makers, on the other hand, are entities that provide liquidity to the market by being willing to buy and sell securities at all times. They do this by quoting bid and ask prices, thus facilitating trades and ensuring that there is always a buyer or seller available. Market makers profit from the spread between the bid and ask prices and play a vital role in maintaining the smooth functioning of financial markets. Their activity helps reduce the gap between buying and selling prices, making it easier for investors to enter and exit positions.

High-frequency trading (HFT) was a significant focus during the event. HFT involves the use of powerful computers and algorithms to execute a large number of orders at extremely high speeds. These trades can occur in fractions of a second, far faster than a human trader could react. HFT firms use these algorithms to take advantage of small price discrepancies in the market, executing trades that may last only a fraction of a second. The sheer volume and speed of these trades can have a profound impact on market liquidity and efficiency.
